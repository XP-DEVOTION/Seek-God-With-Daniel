Welcome to Day 11 of this 21-day journey.

Today’s theme is **When Prayer Feels Dry**.



Our Scripture comes from Psalm 63, verse 1.

Hear the honest cry of the psalmist:

“My soul thirsts for you; my flesh faints for you.”


There are seasons when prayer feels alive and full of warmth.  
And there are seasons when it feels dry—  
words don’t come easily,  
the heart feels distant,  
and God seems quiet.

This dryness is not a sign of failure.  
It is not punishment.  
It is an invitation to a deeper kind of trust—  
trust that goes beyond feelings,  
trust that chooses to stay even when nothing seems to happen.

God is still near in the dryness.  
He honors the prayer you offer even when it feels empty.

Today, you are invited to keep showing up,  
simply because He is worth it.



Here is today’s reflection:

Dryness in prayer is not failure.  
It is an invitation to trust God beyond feelings.



Let us pray together.  
You may speak these words softly or hold them in your heart:

Jesus,  
stay with me in the dryness.  
Teach me to trust You.  
Amen.


Now, a gentle activity for today.

Set aside two minutes—perhaps right now—  
and pray, even if it feels dry.

You don’t need words.  
You don’t need feelings.  
Simply sit in God’s presence.  
Breathe slowly.  
If a word or two comes, offer it.  
If nothing comes, offer the silence.

Offer the time itself to God  
as your prayer.



Thank you for staying.  
That time was not wasted.  
It was received—fully—by a God who never leaves you,  
even in the dry places.

You are not alone.



(Approximate length: 4:30–5:00 minutes, including the two-minute prayer silence)
