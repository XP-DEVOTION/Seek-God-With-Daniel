Welcome to Day 8 of this 21-day journey.

Today’s theme is **Naming What Distracts You**.


Our Scripture comes from Psalm 139, verse 23.

Hear these tender words from the psalmist:

“Search me, O God, and know my heart.”


God already knows everything about you—  
every thought, every pull, every distraction.  
Yet He invites you to name those things yourself,  
not to shame you,  
but to free you.

You cannot release what you have not named.  
When you bring a distraction into the light—  
a habit, a worry, a screen, a fear—  
God meets you there with gentleness,  
ready to help you loosen its grip.

Today is an invitation to honesty before a loving God.

Here is today’s reflection:

You cannot release what you have not named.  
God gently reveals what pulls your heart away.


Let us pray together.  
You may say these words quietly or simply hold them in your heart:

God,  
show me what distracts me from You.  
Give me courage to face it.  
Amen.


Now, a simple but brave activity for today.

Take a moment—perhaps right now—  
and ask the Holy Spirit to bring one distraction to mind.

It might be something obvious, or something subtle.  
When it comes,  
write it down if you can,  
or simply name it silently before God.

No need to fix it today.  
Just name it.  
Offer it.  
Trust that God receives it with mercy.

This small act of honesty is powerful.  
It is the beginning of freedom.

You are safe here with God.  
He is not surprised.  
He is not disappointed.  
He is drawing you closer.
