Welcome to Day 7 of this 21-day journey.

Today’s theme is **Resting in God’s Presence**.


Our Scripture comes from the Gospel of Matthew, chapter 11, verse 28.

Listen to the tender invitation of Jesus:

“Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest.”


Rest is not laziness.  
Rest is not something you earn after you have done enough.

Rest is trust.  
It is the quiet choice to stop striving,  
to lay down the weight you carry,  
and to receive the love that is already yours.

Jesus does not say, “Finish your work, then come.”  
He says, “Come now—tired, burdened, exactly as you are—  
and I will give you rest.”

Today, you are invited to simply come  
and rest in His presence.


Here is today’s reflection:

Rest is not laziness—it is trust.  
God invites you to rest in Him, not to earn His love but to receive it.


Let us pray together.  
You may speak these words softly or hold them in your heart:

Lord,  
give rest to my mind,  
my heart,  
and my body.  
Let me rest in You.  
Amen.


Now, a gentle activity for today.

At some point today—perhaps right now—  
give yourself permission to rest.

Take a quiet walk without hurry.  
Or sit with a warm drink and do nothing else.  
Or simply close your eyes for a few minutes  
and breathe slowly.

Let your body soften.  
Let your thoughts slow.  
Let yourself rest—  
not as a reward,  
but as an act of receiving God’s love.


You do not need to be productive right now.  
You only need to be loved.  
And you already are.
