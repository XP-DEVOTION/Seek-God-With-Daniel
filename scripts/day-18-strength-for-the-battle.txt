Welcome to Day 18 of this 21-day journey.

Today’s theme is **Strength for the Battle**.

Our Scripture comes from Paul’s letter to the Ephesians, chapter 6, verse 10.

Listen to these empowering words:

“Be strong in the Lord and in the strength of his power.”


Spiritual battles are real.  
You feel them in the weariness,  
the opposition,  
the inner struggles,  
the unseen warfare that tries to pull you away from peace.

But you do not fight with your own limited strength.  
You fight from a place of victory already won.  
God Himself equips you.  
His strength becomes your armor,  
His power your weapon,  
His presence your shield.

Today, you are invited to stand—not in your own might,  
but in the unstoppable strength of the Lord.


Here is today’s reflection:

Spiritual battles are real, but God equips you.  
His strength becomes your armor.

Let us pray together.  
You may speak these words boldly or hold them in your heart:

Lord,  
strengthen me for whatever I face today.  
Amen.


Now, a simple but powerful activity for today.

Whenever you feel the weight of the battle today—  
a moment of anxiety,  
tiredness,  
temptation,  
or opposition—  
pause for just a few seconds  
and pray these quiet words:

“Strengthen me, Lord.”

Say it once, or repeat it as needed.  
Let it be your breath prayer,  
your declaration of dependence on His power.


You are not fighting alone.  
The One who is mighty lives in you.  
His strength is made perfect in your weakness.  
Stand firm.  
You are already covered.
