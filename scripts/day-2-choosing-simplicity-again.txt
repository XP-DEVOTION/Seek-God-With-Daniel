Welcome to Day 2 of this 21-day journey.

Today’s theme is **Choosing Simplicity Again**.


Our Scripture is from Daniel, chapter 10, verse 3.

Hear these words:

“I ate no rich food, no meat or wine entered my mouth.”


Daniel didn’t choose extreme deprivation.  
He chose simplicity.  
He set aside the things that were good, but not necessary,  
so his heart could be clearer, lighter, more open to God.

Today, you are invited into the same gentle practice:  
to choose less, not out of punishment,  
but so that you can receive more of what truly matters.


Here is today’s reflection:

Daniel’s practice was simple, not extreme.  
Simplicity clears the heart.  
Today is an invitation to choose less so you can receive more.


Let us pray together.  
You may speak these words aloud or hold them quietly in your heart:

Jesus,  
free me from what distracts me.  
Help me choose simplicity with peace, not pressure.  
Amen.


Now, a simple activity for today.

Take a moment—perhaps right now—and ask yourself:  
What is one small thing I can simplify today?  
It could be your schedule… your space… or one of your habits.

It doesn’t need to be big.  
Just one small, gentle choice.


Name it quietly to yourself, or write it down if you can.

Offer that small choice to God as an act of trust.


You are doing well just by showing up today.  
God meets you in these small, honest steps.


(Approximate length: 2:30–3:00 minutes)
