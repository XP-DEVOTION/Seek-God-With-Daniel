Welcome to Day 9 of this 21-day journey.

Today’s theme is **Letting Go of False Strength**.


Our Scripture comes from Paul’s second letter to the Corinthians, chapter 12, verse 9.

Listen to the words Jesus spoke to Paul:

“My grace is sufficient for you, for power is made perfect in weakness.”


You do not have to be strong for God.  
You do not have to hold it all together,  
pretend you are fine,  
or carry the weight alone.

God is not waiting for you to become stronger  
before He draws near.  
He meets you right in your weakness—  
your tiredness,  
your doubt,  
your limits—  
and there, His power gently fills the space you open.

Today is an invitation to let go of false strength  
and rest in the strength that is already yours in Him.


Here is today’s reflection:

You don’t have to be strong for God.  
He meets you in weakness and fills you with His strength.


Let us pray together.  
You may speak these words softly or hold them silently:

Lord,  
I let go of pretending.  
Be my strength.  
Amen.


Now, a gentle activity for today.

Take a quiet moment—perhaps right now—  
and bring to mind one area where you feel weak today.

It might be physical tiredness,  
emotional strain,  
a struggle you’re facing,  
or simply feeling overwhelmed.

Name it quietly before God.  
You don’t need to explain or fix it.  
Just tell Him:  
“This is where I am weak today.”

Then breathe out slowly and say in your heart:  
“Be my strength.”


He hears you.  
His grace is already sufficient.  
You are not alone in your weakness—  
you are held.
