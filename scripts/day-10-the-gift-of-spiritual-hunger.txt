Welcome to Day 10 of this 21-day journey.

Today’s theme is **The Gift of Spiritual Hunger**.


Our Scripture comes from Psalm 42, verse 1.

Listen to the heartfelt cry of the psalmist:

“As the deer longs for streams of water, so my soul longs for you, O God.”



That deep ache you sometimes feel in your soul—  
the restlessness,  
the quiet longing for something more—  
it is not a problem to fix.

It is a gift.

Spiritual hunger is God’s gentle way  
of drawing you closer,  
of reminding you that you were made for Him.

Even when the longing feels vague or faint,  
it is precious to God.  
It is the bridge that leads you home.

Today, you are invited to receive your hunger as a gift  
and let it pull you toward the One who satisfies.


Here is today’s reflection:

Spiritual hunger is not a problem—it is a gift.  
It draws you toward God.


Let us pray together.  
You may speak these words softly or hold them in your heart:

Lord,  
deepen my longing for You.  
Let my hunger lead me closer.  
Amen.


Now, a gentle activity for today.

The next time you sit down for a meal or take a drink—  
pause for just a moment before you begin.

Notice the physical hunger or thirst in your body.

Then, quietly thank God  
for the deeper hunger of your soul  
that only He can fill.

Offer that soul-hunger to Him  
as a simple, wordless prayer.


This longing you carry is beautiful to God.  
He planted it there on purpose.  
He will not leave it unanswered.

You are closer than you think.
